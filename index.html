The Problem

The Carbon Crisis

The world faces an urgent climate crisis due to the excessive accumulation of greenhouse gases (GHGs) in the atmosphere. Carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and other GHGs contribute to global warming, rising sea levels, extreme weather events, and ecosystem disruptions. The burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes release vast amounts of CO₂ into the air, exacerbating the problem.

Lack of Effective Carbon Capture Solutions

Traditional carbon capture and storage (CCS) methods have limitations. They are often expensive, energy-intensive, and require geological formations for long-term storage. Additionally, existing CCS technologies are not scalable enough to address the magnitude of the problem. We need innovative solutions that can capture and sequester carbon at a gigaton scale to mitigate climate change effectively.

Ocean acidification

The world's oceans are facing an unprecedented crisis due to the rapid increase in ocean acidification. At this rate, the pH of the surface ocean are projected to be under 7.8 by 2100, making it over 150% more acidic compared to today's already-corrosive state. This has severe consequences on marine life. 14% of global coral reefs have died since 2008, and species like clownfish have 5-9 times higher mortality rates in acidic areas.

Our Solution

AlgaZero capitalizes on the carbon-capturing abilities of microalgae

We grow vast quantities of microalgae in open-air pond-based systems on coastal land. These algae can sequester up to 30 times more carbon from the atmosphere than the same area of forest. This permanantly removes CO2 from the atmosphere while also deacidifying vasat amounts of seawater.

AlgaZero will draw 2 billion tonnes of carbon dioxide annually while restoring alkalinity to ocean ecosystems
